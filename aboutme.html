I am a PhD candidate in the Lloyd-Smith Lab in UCLA's Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology.

I study the dynamics of viral emergence from animal reservoirs into new host species. I am broadly interested in how host behavior, demography and immune history influence evolution, competition and coexistence of viruses.

I use models and computation to squeeze the most information from difficult-to-collect epidemiological data and data on animal disease.

In my free time I like to trail run, cycle, cook and backpack in the Sierra Nevada.

See my research page for details on my work.
